Basmati Rice

White rice of the variety known as basmati is frequently farmed in India, which provides almost two-thirds of global consumption. It is a long-grain rice with a delicate nutty taste and floral scent.

Long grain aromatic rice known as "1121 Sella Basmati Rice" has been farmed for many generations in the Indian subcontinent. It is endowed with extra-long, slender grains that elongate at least twice their original size and have a characteristic soft, fluffy texture when cooked. Indian hybrid basmati rice is known as Pusa Basmati. With 70% of the nation's total basmati rice cultivation, it is currently the most widely grown kind. Long grains, a pleasing scent, and a longer shelf life are just a few of the variety's remarkable Basmati rice qualities. Although being a traditional side dish and comfort meal, rice is heavy in carbohydrates and has a place in a healthy diet.

Non–Basmati Rice

Rice other than basmati rice is called non- basmati rice . There are over 10,000 different types of rice in existence, with India having the most. Barely 1% of the rice farmed in India is basmati rice. There are a wide variety of sizes and forms available for non-basmati rice. Some are like beads, others are short and thick, some are long and slender, and some may even be circular. None have the same qualities as basmati rice. There are some varieties of non-basmati rice with names like PR, 104 IR8, IR 64, and Masuri that resemble basmati rice

Jaggery powder

Jaggery powder is a healthy and all-natural alternative to refined sugar that has a rich and complex flavor, making it an excellent choice for elevating any dish. It is made from pure sugarcane juice without any chemicals or additives, making it a nutritious sweetener that retains all the essential vitamins and minerals of sugarcane. With high levels of iron, calcium, magnesium, and potassium, jaggery powder is a rich source of essential nutrients that are vital for maintaining a healthy body. It is also rich in antioxidants, which help boost immunity and combat harmful free radicals.

One of the unique features of jaggery powder is its versatility. It can be used in sweet desserts or savory dishes, adding depth and complexity to any cuisine. Its rich flavor profile makes it an indispensable ingredient in any kitchen, ideal for those seeking a natural and healthy way to sweeten their food. Choosing jaggery powder also demonstrates a commitment to sustainability and ethical practices. Produced using traditional methods that have been passed down for generations, it supports local communities and helps preserve the environment.

Jaggery powder has numerous health benefits as well. It aids digestion, reduces inflammation, and improves skin and hair health, making it an excellent choice for those seeking to maintain their overall well-being. In conclusion, jaggery powder is a natural, versatile, and nutritious sweetener that offers a range of benefits. Whether you are a health-conscious individual or a culinary enthusiast, incorporating jaggery powder into your cooking is a choice that will enhance your palate and nourish your body.
